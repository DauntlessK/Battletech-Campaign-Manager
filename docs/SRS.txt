Software Requirement specification: Battletech Campaign Manager

==================
==   OVERVIEW   ==
==================

1. INTRODUCTION

1.1 Purpose

The purpose of this web app is to help players manage / run their battletech classic campaign. The platform, built upon React, will allow players to build forces with battletech units,
create campaigns, assign a force to a campaign and manage that campaign.

It is designed not only to assist in the normal chaos campaign management - which has other competitiors - it is primarily designed to bridge a gap between running a simplified
campaign like the chaos system, and running a campaign on MekHQ. This middleground is designed to give a bit more interesting depth to a campaign while still being easy to maintain.

1.2 Scope

The Campaign Manager will:

• Registered users can add friends, create & customize forces of various units and pilots, and create / manage / and partipate in campaigns
• Facilitate the running of a campaign by managing the player's resources and unit's status persistance
• Allow a few various types of campaigns to suit player's desired depth / complexity
• Within a campaign, allow a player to log a battle and thus record damage to units and track outcomes as they relate to the campaign

Techstack:

• React front end + tailwind
• Backend Flask? REST endpoints + server rendering?
• MySQL db

1.3

Definitions and Abbreviations

Battletech: A tabletop game from 1984 played on hex-based maps with many different types of units, most usually with mechs

Classic: Only supported game-type by the app, uses BV to balance, game played on hexes, as opposed to Alpha-Strike which is played without hexes (rulers, etc) and PV to balance

Mechs / Meks: an armored combat vehicle of roughly humanoid shape, standing 8 to 14 meters tall and typically massing from 20 to 100 tons.

BV: Battlevalue - used to balance units

Player: A partipant in a battle or campaign

Campaign: A colletion of battles fought with the same, finite units with assets and consequences carrying over from one to the next

Force: A set of units and pilots to be used in a campaign, built around restrictions like BV limit and era

4.0 CAMPAIGNS

Players partipate in one of 3 different campaign types with varying granularity / complexity. Each campaign has one of several settings that can be adjusted upon creation. They include:

Campaign Settings:
-Campaign type
-Campaign sub-type???
-Campaign Scoring
-Campaign era
-Rules Level
-Campaign Force BV limit
-[conquest only] Lances/Stars
-Victory Condition(s)

Some of these settings may be inherently decided by the campaign type selected.

4.1 Campaign Types

- CHAOS CAMPAIGN (2-10p) - The simplest option, an implementation of the chaos campaign system. Extremely simplified for quick and easy management and minimal admin and upkeep, the
campaign uses a generic warchest currency to handle all rearm and resupply costs, unit purchases and sales, upgrades and more. The admin configures the campaign (setting things like
BV / size, warchest size, era, fog of war, etc) and players battle at their own pace. After a battle, both players enter the results and the system will "marry" and verify the data as 
one battle entry (ensuring accuracy between the two records) that can be referenced at any time. Between battles players can spend warchest to repair, etc.

- ADVANCED CAMPAIGN (2-10p) - An advanced campaign is esentially a halfway point between a chaos campaign and the full campaign experience within cam-ops that is in MekHQ. It is designed to
be grittier and more detailed than a chaos campaign but still be appraochable and managable within a faster timeline. Players manage cBills, individual pilot XP, part availabilty and time 
(as a resource for repairs) The key concept is that units like mechs and such will bear true scars and players will truly feel like each battle is a part of a larger conflict. 
Pilots may enter injured already, mechs may be missing armor or whole limbs / weapons, and more as unit attrition is front and center, making for engaging long-term struggle.

- CONQUEST CAMPAIGN (2p) - This strict 2p campaign takes the advanced campaign rules and adds a map and turn layer to it. Players fight over a planet with a full hex-map, which starts
with multiple objectives all hidden across the map. The campaign takes place over a number of turns (either a preset limit, or unlimited) that consist of multiple days. Each turn, players
will prioritize a repair and select a target hex to scout / control etc. Forces may be able to scout more hexes, or devote more time to repair, etc. 

4.2 Campaign Scoring

-While the campaign type determines the depth of management that goes into the player's force- in terms of resources, repairs, and orders, the scoring determines how the campaign is run and
how players are ranked / doing in their campaign and ultimately who wins and loses the campaign. 

Victory Conditions / scoring

- [Chaos, Advanced, Conquest 2P] Total # of battles
- [All Types] Surrender / Automatic Capitulation based on BV or resources (warchest, c-bills)
- [Chaos, Advanced Campaigns] Domination - Over 75% control of objective world / area
- [Conquest Campaigns] Control of key objectives across the world
- [Conquest Campaigns] Finding and eliminating the enemy force's base / dropship
- [Advanced Campaigns, Conquest Campaigns] Time / turns elapsed

4.6 Lances/Stars

Only applicable to conquest campaign. Because each player must assign orders to each lance/star individually, forces should be organized into lances/stars. While within a campaign,
a player can reorganize / switch out mechs etc, the campaign should start off with players having the same amount of lances/stars. Typically this would starting at company-strength,
with 3 lances of 4 mechs each.

4.7 Turns / Time

A turn for advanced / Conquest campaigns can be configured to some degree. A turn represents a set amount of time, usually 1 to 5 days.

For advanced campaigns, a turn is conducted in between battles. Following a battle, a player can use his turn to repair, rearm, and spend resources for other things like upgrades, etc. The
campaign's configured turn length in days will dictate just how much work can be done to repair, rearm and reinforce.

For conquest cmapaigns, a turn is a length of time in which each player will assign orders and those orders are carried out. Each lance/star is given an order like patrol, defend, attack,
repair and it performs that action over that turn. (See section 4.9 for more details)

There are no turns or concept of time in chaos campaigns- battles are fought and then units are repaired as resources are spent between battles.

SCORING

As it relates to chaos and advanced campaigns, the campaign owner sets a number of objectives (1-20 objectives total) that are fought over during the campaign. The campaign owner is also
responsible for setting / fine tuning (or using default values) for battles. At its most basic, every battle can be worth a specific percentage of control over an objective. In 2p+ campaigns
this would also mean a loss in control for the loser as well as a gain for the winner. Depending on the type of battle (and maybe results / # of losses / margin?) the amount won and the
amount lost may differ.

Certain objectives, with control over a threshold (75%?) may grant additional bonuses. Such as: An additional tech (for repairs), a cash income (one time or after each battle), 
a radar or comms facility, etc

The web app is designed to facilitate several different methods of running a classic battletech campaign. Registered users can add friends, create forces consisting of
various units and pilots, and create campaigns. The main purpose of the program is to do the heavy lifting for various level campaigns, from simpler ones to more
advanced ones, taking care of unit management (keeping track of unit's damage, repairs, etc), XP and resource expenditure and more. All campaigns share some common features:

• Force management between battles (repair, rearmament, purchasing new/replacement units, XP expenditure)
• Battle logs (a history of all battles fought in the campaign, with results in each, keeping track of wins, losses, participants, kills, and more)
• Resource Tracking (warchest / cBills, etc)

like the user's very own unit, from name, unit insignia and TO&E, it should be easy to create the unit and build (add units, etc).
